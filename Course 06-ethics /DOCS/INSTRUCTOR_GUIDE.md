# Instructor Guide - AIAT 116
## دليل المدرس - AIAT 116

### Quick Start Guide

This curriculum is designed for teaching Ethics of Artificial Intelligence. All materials are bilingual (Arabic/English).

---

## Course Structure Overview

### Unit 1: Foundations of AI Ethics
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks

**Key Topics:**
- Ethical frameworks (utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics)
- Historical context of AI ethics
- Ethical decision-making frameworks
- Key ethical principles and values

**Files:**
- `unit1-ethics-foundations/examples/` - Code examples and case studies
- `unit1-ethics-foundations/exercises/` - Practice problems
- `unit1-ethics-foundations/solutions/` - Complete solutions
- `unit1-ethics-foundations/quizzes/` - Assessment quizzes

**Teaching Tips:**
1. Start with foundational ethical theories to establish common vocabulary
2. Use historical case studies to illustrate ethical dilemmas
3. Encourage discussion and debate on ethical scenarios
4. Connect theoretical frameworks to practical AI applications
5. Assign exercises that require ethical reasoning

---

### Unit 2: Bias, Justice, and Discrimination in AI
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks

**Key Topics:**
- Types of bias in AI systems (algorithmic, data, human)
- Fairness metrics (demographic parity, equalized odds, calibration)
- Algorithmic discrimination and its impacts
- Bias detection and mitigation techniques

**Files:**
- `unit2-bias-justice/examples/` - Bias detection and fairness analysis examples
- `unit2-bias-justice/exercises/` - Practice problems
- `unit2-bias-justice/solutions/` - Complete solutions
- `unit2-bias-justice/quizzes/` - Assessment quizzes

**Teaching Tips:**
1. Use real-world examples of biased AI systems (COMPAS, facial recognition)
2. Demonstrate bias detection using tools like fairlearn or aif360
3. Show how different fairness metrics can conflict
4. Discuss trade-offs in bias mitigation
5. Encourage students to analyze datasets for potential bias

---

### Unit 3: Privacy, Security, and Data Protection
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks

**Key Topics:**
- Data privacy principles and regulations
- Security vulnerabilities in AI systems
- Privacy-preserving techniques (differential privacy, federated learning)
- GDPR and data protection regulations

**Files:**
- `unit3-privacy-security/examples/` - Privacy and security examples
- `unit3-privacy-security/exercises/` - Practice problems
- `unit3-privacy-security/solutions/` - Complete solutions
- `unit3-privacy-security/quizzes/` - Assessment quizzes

**Teaching Tips:**
1. Explain privacy vs. utility trade-offs
2. Demonstrate differential privacy with code examples
3. Discuss real-world data breaches and their implications
4. Review GDPR requirements and their impact on AI development
5. Show how to implement privacy-preserving techniques

---

### Unit 4: Interpretability, Transparency, and Accountability
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks

**Key Topics:**
- Explainable AI (XAI) methods (SHAP, LIME, feature importance)
- Model interpretability and transparency
- Accountability frameworks
- Right to explanation

**Files:**
- `unit4-transparency-accountability/examples/` - Interpretability examples
- `unit4-transparency-accountability/exercises/` - Practice problems
- `unit4-transparency-accountability/solutions/` - Complete solutions
- `unit4-transparency-accountability/quizzes/` - Assessment quizzes

**Teaching Tips:**
1. Demonstrate SHAP and LIME with real models
2. Show the difference between global and local interpretability
3. Discuss when interpretability is most critical
4. Explore trade-offs between model complexity and interpretability
5. Use case studies where lack of transparency caused problems

---

### Unit 5: AI Governance, Regulations, and Future Challenges
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks

**Key Topics:**
- AI governance models and frameworks
- Regulatory frameworks (GDPR, EU AI Act, etc.)
- Industry standards and best practices
- Future ethical challenges in AI

**Files:**
- `unit5-governance-regulations/examples/` - Governance examples
- `unit5-governance-regulations/exercises/` - Practice problems
- `unit5-governance-regulations/solutions/` - Complete solutions
- `unit5-governance-regulations/quizzes/` - Assessment quizzes

**Teaching Tips:**
1. Compare different regulatory approaches globally
2. Discuss the role of industry self-regulation
3. Explore emerging ethical challenges (AGI, autonomous systems)
4. Encourage students to develop their own governance frameworks
5. Connect governance to all previous units

---

## Teaching Methodology

### 1. **Case Study Approach**
- Use real-world examples throughout
- Analyze ethical dilemmas from multiple perspectives
- Encourage critical thinking and debate

### 2. **Hands-On Analysis**
- Use code examples to demonstrate concepts
- Have students analyze AI systems for ethical issues
- Practice with bias detection and fairness tools

### 3. **Discussion-Based Learning**
- Facilitate classroom discussions on ethical scenarios
- Encourage diverse viewpoints
- Connect theory to practice

### 4. **Progressive Complexity**
- Start with foundational concepts
- Build complexity gradually
- Integrate concepts across units

---

## Assessment Strategy

### Formative Assessment
- **Quizzes:** Short assessments after each unit (15-30 minutes)
- **Exercises:** Practice problems with solutions
- **Discussions:** In-class ethical scenario analysis

### Summative Assessment
- **Unit Tests:** Comprehensive tests after each unit
- **Case Study Analysis:** Real-world ethical analysis projects
- **Final Exam:** Comprehensive exam covering all units

### Grading Distribution (Suggested)
- Quizzes: 20%
- Exercises: 20%
- Case Studies: 30%
- Final Exam: 30%

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Challenge 1: Abstract Concepts
**Solution:** Use concrete examples and case studies to illustrate abstract ethical principles.

### Challenge 2: Diverse Perspectives
**Solution:** Encourage respectful debate and acknowledge that ethical questions often have no single "right" answer.

### Challenge 3: Technical vs. Ethical Balance
**Solution:** Integrate technical examples with ethical analysis, showing how they inform each other.

### Challenge 4: Keeping Content Current
**Solution:** Regularly update with recent case studies and emerging regulations.

## Bilingual Teaching Tips

### Arabic/English Integration
- Present key terms in both languages
- Use Arabic for cultural context and local examples
- Provide bilingual code comments
- Support students in their preferred language

### Cultural Sensitivity
- Acknowledge different cultural perspectives on ethics
- Include examples relevant to the region
- Respect diverse viewpoints
